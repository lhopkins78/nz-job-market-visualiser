# 🎯 Key Findings: NZ Jobs AI Exposure Analysis

## Quick Summary

**57 NZ occupations analyzed covering 1.8 million jobs**

- **Average AI Exposure:** 4.8/10 (Moderate)
- **Range:** 1/10 (Firefighters) to 10/10 (Data Entry)
- **High Risk (8-10):** 6 occupations (~180K people)
- **Low Risk (0-3):** 21 occupations (~560K people)
- **Moderate Risk (4-7):** 30 occupations (~1.08M people)

## 💡 Patterns & Insights

### 1. **The Digital Divide**
- **Purely digital work:** Highly exposed (8-10)
- **Purely physical work:** Low exposure (0-3)
- **Mixed work:** Moderate exposure (4-7)

### 2. **Wage Paradox**
High-paying roles often score high because they're digital:
- Software Dev ($1,850/wk) = 8/10 exposure
- Lawyer ($1,850/wk) = 8/10 exposure
- Data Analyst ($1,750/wk) = 8/10 exposure

But not all high-wage jobs are exposed:
- Doctor ($1,950/wk) = 6/10 (physical diagnosis + procedures)
- Firefighter ($1,620/wk) = 1/10 (physical + lives at risk)

### 3. **Low-Risk Occupations Are Growing**
- Aged care workers: +growth (aging population)
- Childcare workers: +growth
- Construction trades: ~stable

### 4. **High-Risk Occupations Are Shrinking**
- Secretaries/Receptionists: -decline
- Data Entry Operators: -decline
- Retail Salespersons: -decline (e-commerce)

### 5. **NZ-Specific Factors**
- **Remote work adoption:** Increases "digital exposure" by making everything screen-based
- **Digital maturity:** NZ businesses increasingly automated
- **Sector concentration:** Healthcare & construction large (safer sectors)
- **Labor law:** Strict employment law slows automation vs. other countries

## ❓ FAQs

**Q: Does this mean these jobs will disappear?**
A: No. It means these roles will *change*. People will shift to higher-value work (strategy, relationships, creativity) while AI handles routine tasks.

**Q: Software Dev scores 8/10 but these jobs are growing. Why?**
A: High exposure ≠ job loss. Rather, the job changes. Software devs will spend less time on routine coding (AI helps), more on architecture/design/strategy.

**Q: Should I be worried if my role scores 7/10?**
A: Not panicked, but alert. Focus on developing skills AI can't do: judgment, relationships, creativity, leadership.

**Q: What can organizations do?**
A: 1) Upskill workers in exposed roles. 2) Create career paths from routine to strategic work. 3) Invest in human skills (leadership, judgment, creativity). 4) Plan workforce transitions proactively.
